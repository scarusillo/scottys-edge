The Math Check

Calibration

"20% edge" is a claim. Calibration is the test. If our 20% edge picks hit at 55% win rate, the edge is real. If they hit at 48%, the edge is a story we're telling ourselves.

Every row below is computed from graded_bets — the same table that powers the dashboard. No filtering for cherry-picked windows. The sample is all picks ≥ 3.5u since March 4, 2026 (the start of the post-rebuild period in the methodology sense).

How to read this: "Actual WR" is our real win rate. "Implied WR" is what the market odds implied we should win. "Delta" is the difference — positive means we beat the market's implied probability (real edge). We track this because win rate alone doesn't prove anything — you have to compare against what the market priced in.

What this tells us

The edge is real above 20%. The 20–22% and 22–25% buckets both show meaningful positive delta vs the implied probability. That's the mathematical test of whether "edge" is just a marketing word or an actual measurable quantity. In our data, it's measurable.

The 16–20% bucket confirms the 20% floor. Actual WR below the firing threshold is slightly worse than implied, which is why we don't fire there. Moving the floor below 20% would likely be P/L-negative on our current data.

NHL is carrying the sport breakdown post-rebuild. A transparent operator should say when results are concentrated in one sport, and they are. If the NHL signal weakens, the model's profitability could stall until another sport's cohort recovers.

The 25%+ bucket is calibrated but has a visible tail. +2.3% delta is still positive but tighter than 22–25%. Most of that tail was pre-v25.13 longshot props. Post-v25.13, the 25%+ bucket's future composition will be different from its historical composition.

Sample sizes still matter. The moneyline numbers (80% WR, +15.7u) are cherry-picked by an n=5 sample. Don't read conviction into anything under n=20.
